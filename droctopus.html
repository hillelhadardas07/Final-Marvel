He first appeared in "The Amazing Spider-Man" #3 in 1963 and has since become one of Spider-Man's most iconic and enduring adversaries. Doctor Octopus is often portrayed as a brilliant scientist and inventor, specializing in mechanical engineering and robotics.

In his original origin story, Dr. Otto Octavius was a respected and highly intelligent nuclear physicist. During an experiment gone wrong, his mechanical tentacle apparatus, which he designed to manipulate hazardous materials, became fused to his body. This accident also caused a mental breakdown, leading Octavius to adopt the persona of Doctor Octopus. The mechanical arms, or "tentacles," are permanently attached to his back and give him superhuman strength, the ability to climb walls, and incredible dexterity.

Doctor Octopus is known for his relentless pursuit of power and scientific knowledge. He has a keen intellect and is often portrayed as one of Spider-Man's most formidable foes. He has a history of criminal activities, including bank robberies, extortion, and acts of terrorism. Although he lacks the physical abilities of some other supervillains, his mechanical arms make him a formidable opponent in battle.

Over the years, Doctor Octopus has played a significant role in several key storylines in Spider-Man comics. One of the most notable instances was the "Spider-Man No More!" storyline in "The Amazing Spider-Man" #50, where he discovers Spider-Man's secret identity and launches a personal vendetta against him. In the famous storyline "The Clone Saga," Doctor Octopus switches minds with Peter Parker, leaving Peter trapped in Octavius' dying body while Octavius lives on as Spider-Man, leading to a dramatic and controversial twist in the series.

Doctor Octopus has also appeared in various adaptations outside the comics. He is a recurring character in animated TV shows featuring Spider-Man, such as "Spider-Man: The Animated Series" and "The Spectacular Spider-Man." In the live-action Spider-Man films, he was portrayed by Alfred Molina in the 2004 film "Spider-Man 2" and later reprised the role in the 2021 film "Spider-Man: No Way Home." The character has become a fan favorite due to his complex motivations and his dynamic with Spider-Man.
